The user is from northern Mexico. His own writing uses tú-form ("puedes", "tienes", "gustaría", "avísame") and neutral Mexican-Spanish vocabulary. He does not use voseo in daily life, and voseo forms felt out of place to him — he asked about it out of curiosity on 2026-07-06 after noticing I had been using them.

**Why:** He explicitly said "me da igual" so this is not a hard rule. But matching his register removes friction and keeps the tone consistent with his own writing.

**How to apply:** Default to tú-form Spanish imperatives ("corre", "abre", "prueba", "revisa", "dime", "pégame"), neutral vocabulary, no voseo verb forms ("arrancá", "decíme", "pegáme", "corré", "andás") and no Argentine-flavored terms ("acá" → "aquí" is OK either way; "chévere" → don't use). English remains the language for code, commits, PR descriptions, and docs per the frontend CLAUDE.md.
